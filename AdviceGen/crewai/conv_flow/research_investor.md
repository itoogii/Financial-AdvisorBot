# Comprehensive User Research: US & UK Retail Stock Market Investors

## 1. Overview

This research aims to deliver a deep understanding of the diverse segments of stock market retail investors in the US and UK. The goal is to inform the development of rich, realistic personas for the financial advisor chatbot, ensuring broader market fit and impact. This research covers demographics, financial literacy, investment goals, technology comfort, behaviors, preferences, and pain points across four generational cohorts: Gen Z, Millennials, Gen X, and Boomers.

### b. Additional Demographics

- **Gender:** Historically male-dominated, but increasing participation from women and non-binary investors.
- **Income Brackets:** Widely distributed—from students/entry-level (Gen Z) to top-earners/retirees (Boomers).
- **Education:** Range from high school diploma (especially in older/blue-collar Gen X or Boomers) to graduate-level qualification (common among Millennials and younger Gen X).
- **Location:** Urban and suburban concentrations; rural engagement increasing due to app accessibility.
- **Ethnicity:** Diverse, with growing participation from minority groups, especially among younger cohorts.
- **Occupation:** Wide spectrum—students, professionals, self-employed, retired.

## 3. Financial Literacy Levels

- **Low:** Struggle with basic concepts, rely heavily on intuitive, non-research-based decisions; common with younger or first-time investors.
- **Medium:** Basic understanding of stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, and risk; seeks simple tools and clear explanations.
- **High:** Familiar with fundamental and technical analysis, portfolio strategy, tax implications; often research-driven investors.

Distribution:

- **Gen Z:** Primarily low to medium; exposure through social media/finfluencers.
- **Millennials:** Medium to high; eager to self-educate, active learners.
- **Gen X:** Medium to high; experienced, but some gaps due to old-school investment environments.
- **Boomers:** High (if actively invested), but many have static strategies since retirement or near-retirement.

## 6. Investment Behaviors

### a. Gen Z

- **Behavior:** Experiment with small amounts (“learning money”), prefer stocks tied to social/personal causes (ESG), value community insights (Reddit, Discord).
- **Buy/Sell Trigger:** Viral trends, peer recommendations, fear of missing out (FOMO).

### b. Millennials

- **Behavior:** More research-oriented, increasingly moving from “day trading” to strategic long-term investing. Actively uses robo-advisors, ETFs.
- **Buy/Sell Trigger:** Major news, recession fears, fintech education tools, social media.

### c. Gen X

- **Behavior:** Blend traditional and digital behaviors. Diversified portfolios using both managed and self-directed accounts.
- **Buy/Sell Trigger:** Life events (child’s college, divorce), job changes, advice from trusted sources.

### d. Boomers

- **Behavior:** Conservative, prefer blue-chip stocks, high-dividend instruments, or bonds. Less frequent trading, more “buy and hold.”
- **Buy/Sell Trigger:** Market volatility, retirement planning milestones, fear of capital loss.

## 8. Emotional Triggers & Pain Points

### a. Emotional Triggers

- **Security/Trust:** Worry about scams, false information, platform reliability
- **FOMO:** Seeing peers succeed triggers impulsive trading (especially younger generations)
- **Fear of Loss:** Market downturns induce anxiety; strong in Boomers and Gen X
- **Excitement:** “Win” trades increase frequency of trades, risk taking (Gen Z, Millennials)
- **Uncertainty:** Changes in regulations, platform outages, unclear UI/UX

### b. Common Pain Points

- **Complexity of Information:** Overwhelmed by jargon, data, confusing interfaces (across cohorts)
- **Hidden Fees & Charges:** Frustration at unexpected costs (all generations)
- **Difficult Onboarding:** Cumbersome KYC/AML processes or unclear guidance
- **Lack of Personalization:** Advice/tools don't fit their profile, especially for unique goals (e.g., gig workers, self-employed)
- **Risk Assessment Difficulty:** Unclear risk–reward explanation; fear of "gambling" with important funds
- **Fear Around Security and Data Privacy:** Doubts about app safety, data usage, especially rising in the AI age
- **Market Volatility Anxiety:** Not enough guidance during market crises or downturns
- **Decision Paralysis:** Endless options leading to inaction
- **Insufficient Education:** Desire for embedded, just-in-time financial literacy help

## 9. Needs & Preferences by Generation

### Gen Z

- Seamless, mobile-first experiences with social integration
- Bite-sized education, tips, and gamification
- Trusted, community-driven decision support
- Low barriers to entry (fractional shares)
- Desire to invest for impact (ESG options)

### Millennials

- Tools for long-term planning, automated investing (robo-advisors)
- Integration with budgeting and debt management solutions
- Transparent, jargon-free communication
- High-demand for flexibility and self-directed tools
- Responsible investing and outcome tracking

### Gen X

- Guidance for balancing growth and preservation
- Simple aggregation of multiple accounts/portfolios (401k, IRAs, ISAs)
- Planning for college, aging parents, retirement
- Clear risk/reward breakdowns, scenario analysis
- Value strong customer support (human and AI)

### Boomers

- Straightforward interfaces, minimalistic design
- High trust requirements—clear security credentials and data privacy policies
- Wealth transfer and legacy planning tools
- Assurance on capital protection, low-to-no-risk options
- Direct support (phone, simple chat)

   Struggles: Complexity, fragmented advice  
   Literacy: Medium-high

4. **The Preservation Seeker (“Boomer Traditionalist”)**  
   Wants: Income, safety, easy legacy plans  
   Struggles: Tech, fear of loss  
   Literacy: High (investment core), low (new tech)  
   Tech: Low-moderate

## 11. Conclusions & Recommendations

- **Design software and chatbot flows to account for varying literacy:** Layered help, explainers, and “just-in-time” education.
- **Personalization is critical:** Investor onboarding must quickly build a profile (risk, goals, style) for tailored recommendations.
- **Clarity, transparency, and ease of use:** Particularly for fees, account movement, and risk.
- **Emphasize trust, privacy, and security at every turn:** Certify with visible credentials, clear policies.
- **Incorporate behavioral, emotional triggers in chatbot engagement:** E.g., when fear is detected during market swings, shift to support-mode.
- **Facilitate community, social proof, and reliable education:** Especially for younger cohorts.
